How does Golding use symbolism to develop a theme in Lord of the Flies?

Example Analysis

THEME Golding portrays Jack’s descent into savagery and violence through the symbolism of his mask. In chapter four, Jack camouflages his face in order to hunt pigs. He decides to paint his face. So, he “slashed a black bar of charcoal” and “rubbed red over” half of his face. (Golding, 35). After he had finished, “the mask compelled him” and “hid [him] from shame and self-consciousness” (Golding, 36). The word "slashed," rather than words like "rubbed" or "smeared," has connotations with knives and violent behavior in general. Furthermore, the color red connotates with images of blood, and blood, in most cases, gives an impression of violent behavior. Jack is characterized as violent, aggressive, and savage by his association with knives and blood. His violence and aggression contribute to the novel's savage theme. It's also worth noting that masks are frequently used to impersonate others; however, this mask allows Jack to be himself. I believe Jack is motivated solely by his desire for pleasure, with no regard for the consequences of his actions. Jack appears to be a psychopath, in my opinion, because he doesn’t have any emotions towards his violent actions, Jack also represents Hobbes' philosophy of mankind: that man is inherently violent and will act this way in the absence of authority, but I don't believe that Jack or mankind is inherently evil because it depends on the environment, or a better way to say it is man depicts his environment, and the environment on the island is violent and savage, and the only way to live in this environment is through violence, hence, I disagree with some of Hobbes' philosophy, but I believe that without authority, humans will become violent and evil, and this is because there are others in our environment apart from humans, including animals, and these animals are violent and savage; thus, humans imitate their environment. In conclusion, through the violent behavior of Jack and his mask and his descent into violent and savage behavior, Golding is able to develop the theme of savagery in Lord of the Flies.
