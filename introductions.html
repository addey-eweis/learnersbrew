Things To Keep In Mind.

Did you use a hook in your introduction?

Did you use a bridge in your introduction?

Did you use a thesis statement in your introduction?

Introductions

The introduction to an essay is the first thing readers will see, and it's what sets the tone for the entire piece. To grab a reader's attention and prove that your essay deserves their time, you need a good introduction. The introduction should be clear, concise, and insightful; a hook that draws readers in just enough to want to read more.

Hooks

Hooks are the introduction of an argumentative essay. A hook is usually a question or explanation that causes the reader to want to read on. Hooks can also be shocking or surprising statements.

Bridges

The bridge picks up where the thesis leaves off and provides a transition into talking about your main point more specifically, so it can be explained in greater detail for readers.

Thesis Statments

A Thesis statement is usually the first sentence in an essay or the first few sentences in a research paper. It states your opinion on a topic, and it informs your readers of the direction that you'll be heading with the rest of your words. It's what ties everything together, so to speak. Taking out or muddying up your thesis statement can leave you with serious problems later on in your writing process.
